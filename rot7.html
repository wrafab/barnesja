Rotation 7

Creating a Skateboard



This rotation we worked on designing, laminating, and vacuum forming skateboards. I used CorelDraw to create a design for the bottom of my board. Then, using the laser printer, I rastered the design onto a sheet of wood.

Then it was time to assemble the board The process began with 1/16 inch wood sheets. The sheets were ordered by their seam direction in order to provide the board strength and flexibilty needed for a good board.

After that, we took the sheets and laminated them together by pouring a glue onto the surface then spreading it with a roller. The process was then repeated with all the sheets being stacked on one another

Once the pieces were laminated together, we put the board on top of a piece of styrofoam that would be used for molding the board into the proper shape. The two parts were then put into an airtight bag with a strip

of mesh called a breather in between the board and the valve. The bag was sealed with a strip of tar and we began to use a pump to take out the air. Once the pump began clicking, we stopped pumping and left the

board to sit for 24 hours. When I took mine out, it was slightly misshapen due to mistake in positioning the board on the styrofoam. I sanded down the edges of my board in order to make them a bit cleaner looking

After I had sanded the faces of the board, I spray painted the top. After the paint had dried, I applied a protective layer to keep the board looking good even after use
